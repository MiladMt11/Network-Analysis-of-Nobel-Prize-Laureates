'''Alfred Hermann Fried''' ({{IPA-de|ˈʔalfʁeːt ˈhɛʁman ˈfʁiːt}}; 11 November 1864 – 4 May 1921) was an [[Austria]]n [[Jew]]ish [[pacifist]], publicist, journalist, co-founder of the German peace movement, and winner (with [[Tobias Asser]]) of the [[Nobel Peace Prize]] in 1911. Fried was also a supporter of [[Esperanto]]. He is the author of an Esperanto textbook and an Esperanto-German and German-Esperanto dictionary, first published in 1903 and republished in 1905.<ref name=":0" /> 

== Life ==
{{expand section|date=October 2018}}
Born in [[Vienna]], [[Austria-Hungary]], to a Jewish family, Fried left school at the age of 15 and started to work in a bookshop. In 1883 he moved to [[Berlin]], where he opened a bookshop of his own in 1887. Following the publication by [[Bertha von Suttner]] of ''[[Die Waffen nieder]]!'' (''Lay Down Your Arms'') in 1889, he and von Suttner began in 1892 to print a magazine of the same name. In articles published within ''Die Waffen nieder!'' and its successor, ''[[Die Friedenswarte]]'' (''The Peace Watch''), he articulated his pacifist philosophy. 

In 1892 he was a co-founder of the German Peace Society ([[Deutsche Friedensgesellschaft]]). He was one of the fathers of the idea of a modern organisation to assure worldwide peace (the principal idea was developed in the [[League of Nations]] and after the [[Second World War]] in the [[United Nations|UN]]). 

Fried was a prominent member of the [[Esperanto]]-movement. In 1903 he published the book ''Lehrbuch der internationalen Hilfssprache Esperanto'' (''Textbook of the International Language Esperanto'').<ref name=":0" />

In 1909 he collaborated with [[Paul Otlet]] and [[Henri La Fontaine]] of the [[Union of International Associations|Central Office of International Associations]] in the preparation of the ''Annuaire de la Vie Internationale''.<ref name="WELIS">{{cite book |last1=Rayward |first1=W. Boyd |title=World Encyclopedia of Library and Information Services |date=1993 |publisher=American Library Association |isbn=9780838906095 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HSFu99FCJwQC&q=%22Central+Office+of+International+Associations%22&pg=PA643 |access-date=4 November 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

In 1911 he received the [[Nobel Peace Prize]] together with [[Tobias Asser]]. 

During [[World War I]], Fried moved to neutral Switzerland, and continued to advocate international peace. He died in his hometown, Vienna, in 1921.<ref name="Henriksson1938">{{cite book|author=Fritz Henriksson|title=The Nobel prizes and their founder, Alfred Nobel|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AbVRAAAAMAAJ|year=1938|publisher=Printed by A. Bonniers boktryckeri|page=28}}</ref> His ashes are buried at [[Feuerhalle Simmering]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/alfred-fried|title = Alfred Fried}}</ref>
