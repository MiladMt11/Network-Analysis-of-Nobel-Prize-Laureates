'''Karl Sune Detlof Bergström''' (10 January 1916 – 15 August 2004) was a [[Sweden|Swedish]] [[biochemist]]. In 1975, he was appointed to the Nobel Foundation Board of Directors in Sweden,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/medicine/laureates/1982/bergstrom-autobio.html|title=Sune K. Bergström – Biographical|website=nobelprize.org|access-date=17 April 2018}}</ref> and was awarded the [[Louisa Gross Horwitz Prize]] from [[Columbia University]], together with [[Bengt I. Samuelsson]].
He shared the [[Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine]] with [[Bengt I. Samuelsson]] and [[John R. Vane]] in 1982, for discoveries concerning [[prostaglandin]]s and related substances.

Bergström was elected a member of the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences]] in 1965, and its President in 1983. In 1965, he was also elected a member of the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Engineering Sciences]].  He was elected a Foreign Honorary Member of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 1966.<ref name=AAAS>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780–2010: Chapter B|url=http://www.amacad.org/publications/BookofMembers/ChapterB.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=16 June 2011}}</ref> Bergström was awarded the [[Cameron Prize for Therapeutics of the University of Edinburgh]] in 1977. In 1985 he was appointed member of the [[Pontifical Academy of Sciences]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.casinapioiv.va/content/accademia/en/academicians/deceased/bergstrom.html|title=Sune Bergstrom|website=www.casinapioiv.va|access-date=17 April 2018}}</ref>

In 1943, Bergström married Maj Gernandt.<ref>Wright, Pearce (18 August 2004) [https://www.theguardian.com/news/2004/aug/18/guardianobituaries.health Sune Bergstrom]. ''The Guardian''</ref> He had two sons, the businessman [[Rurik Reenstierna]], with Maj Gernandt, and the evolutionary geneticist [[Svante Pääbo]], from an extramarital affair with the Estonian chemist Karin Pääbo. Both sons were born in 1955, and Rurik had learned about existence of Svante only around 2004.<ref>Kolbert, Elizabeth (15 August 2011) [https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2011/08/15/sleeping-with-the-enemy#ixzz1vCPDr4Xw Sleeping with the Enemy]. ''The New Yorker''</ref>
