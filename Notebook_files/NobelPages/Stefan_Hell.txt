'''Stefan Walter Hell''' [[HonFRMS]] ({{IPA-de|ˈʃtɛfan ˈhɛl|-|De-Stefan Hell.ogg}}: born 23 December 1962) is a Romanian-German [[physicist]] and one of the directors of the [[Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry]] in [[Göttingen]], [[Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mpibpc.mpg.de/english/start/index.php|title=Homepage|website=www.mpibpc.mpg.de|access-date=11 June 2017}}</ref> He received the [[Nobel Prize in Chemistry]] in 2014 "for the development of [[Super-resolution microscopy|super-resolved fluorescence microscopy]]", together with [[Eric Betzig]] and [[W.E. Moerner|William Moerner]].<ref name=NP>{{cite web|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2014/press.pdf|title=Nobelprize.org|access-date=11 June 2017}}</ref>

[[File:2color-STED-example.png|thumb|300px| Image of proteins from the nuclear pore complex using STED microscopy]]

== Life ==

Born into a [[Banat Swabians|Banat Swabian]] family in [[Arad, Romania|Arad]], [[Romania]], he grew up at his parents'  home in nearby [[Sântana]].<ref>{{in lang|ro}} Răzvan Băltăreţu, [http://adevarul.ro/tech/stiinta/premiul-nobel-chimie-fost-castigat-roman-1_5435107c0d133766a8c357bf/index.html "Un cercetător născut în judeţul Arad este printre câştigătorii premiului Nobel pentru chimie"], ''Adevărul'', October 8, 2014</ref><ref>Andreea Ofiţeru, [http://www.gandul.info/stiri/nobel-2014-stefan-w-hell-pentru-gandul-am-avut-profesori-extraordinari-in-romania-13373041 "Stefan W. Hell, pentru Gândul: 'Am avut profesori extraordinari în România'"], ''Gândul'', October 9, 2014</ref> Hell attended primary school there between 1969 and 1977.<ref name="poco">{{in lang|ro}} Andreea Pocotila, [http://www.romanialibera.ro/stiinta-tehnologie/stiinta/fizicianul-premiat-cu-nobelul-pentru-chimie-vorbeste-romaneste-si-tine-legatura-cu-mediul-stiintific-din-tara-noastra-352654 "Fizicianul premiat cu Nobelul pentru chimie vorbește românește și ține legătura cu mediul științific din țara noastră"], ''România Liberă'', October 8, 2014</ref> Subsequently, he attended one year of secondary education at the [[Nikolaus Lenau High School]] in [[Timișoara]] before leaving with his parents to [[West Germany]] in 1978.<ref>{{in lang|ro}} Stefan Both, [http://adevarul.ro/locale/timisoara/exclusiv-stefan-whell-castigatorul-nobelului-chimie-fost-elev-liceul-nikolaus-lenau-timisoara-1_54351d5e0d133766a8c39e0e/index.html "Stefan W.Hell, al doilea elev de la Liceul 'Nikolaus Lenau' din Timişoara care a câştigat un Nobel"], ''Adevărul'', October 8, 2014</ref> His father was an engineer and his mother a teacher; the family settled in [[Ludwigshafen]] after emigrating.<ref name="poco"/>

Hell began his studies at the [[Ruprecht Karl University of Heidelberg|Heidelberg University]] in 1981, where he received his [[doctorate]] in physics in 1990. His thesis advisor was the solid-state physicist Siegfried Hunklinger.  The title of the thesis was “Imaging of transparent microstructures in a confocal microscope”.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.deutscher-zukunftspreis.de/newsite/2006/lebenslauf_01.shtml|title=Curriculum Vitae|access-date=11 June 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071025151247/http://www.deutscher-zukunftspreis.de/newsite/2006/lebenslauf_01.shtml|archive-date=25 October 2007}}</ref> He was an independent inventor for a short period thereafter working on improving depth (axial) [[Optical resolution|resolution]] in [[confocal microscopy]], which became later known as the [[4Pi microscope]]. Resolution is the possibility to separate two similar objects in close proximity and is therefore the most important property of a microscope.

From 1991 to 1993 Hell worked at the [[European Molecular Biology Laboratory]] in [[Heidelberg]],<ref name="mpibpc.gwdg.de">{{cite web|url=http://www.mpibpc.gwdg.de/groups/hell/personals/shell.html|title=NanoBiophotonics - Stefan W. Hell's Personal Profile|website=www.mpibpc.gwdg.de|access-date=11 June 2017}}</ref> where he succeeded in demonstrating the principles of 4-Pi microscopy. From 1993 to 1996 he worked as a group leader at the [[University of Turku]] (Finland) in the department for [[Medical Physics]],<ref name="Deutscher Zukunftspreis">{{cite web|url=http://www.deutscher-zukunftspreis.de/newsite/2006/lebenslauf_01.shtml|title=Deutscher Zukunftspreis<!-- Bot generated title -->|access-date=11 June 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071025151247/http://www.deutscher-zukunftspreis.de/newsite/2006/lebenslauf_01.shtml|archive-date=25 October 2007}}</ref> where he developed the principle for stimulated emission depletion [[STED microscopy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mpibpc.mpg.de/groups/pr/PR/2006/06_20/|title=MPI für biophysikalische Chemie: Hell für Deutschen Zukunftspreis 2006 nominiert|website=www.mpibpc.mpg.de|access-date=11 June 2017}}</ref> From 1993 to 1994 Hell was also for 6 months a visiting scientist at the [[University of Oxford]] (England).<ref name="Deutscher Zukunftspreis"/>
He received his [[habilitation]] in physics from the University of Heidelberg in 1996. On 15 October 2002 Hell became a director of the Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry in Göttingen <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mpibpc.gwdg.de/groups/hell/other_publications/Helleinzeln.pdf|title=Max film<!-- Bot generated title -->|access-date=11 June 2017}}</ref> and he established the department of Nanobiophotonics. Since 2003 Hell has also been the leader of the department "Optical Nanoscopy division" at the [[German Cancer Research Center]] (DKFZ) in Heidelberg and "non-budgeted professor" (apl. Prof.) in the [[Heidelberg University Faculty of Physics and Astronomy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leopoldina.org/fileadmin/redaktion/Mitglieder/CV_Hell_Stefan_D.pdf|title=CV of Stefan Hell|publisher=Academy of Sciences Leopoldina|access-date=October 9, 2014}}</ref> Since 2004 he has been an honorary professor for experimental physics at the faculty of physics of the [[University of Göttingen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uni-goettingen.de/en/57981.html|title=Hell, Stefan, Prof. Dr. Dr. h.c. mult.|work= Göttingen Graduate School for Neurosciences, Biophysics, and Molecular Biosciences|access-date=2015-12-03}}</ref>

With the invention and subsequent development of [[STED microscopy|Stimulated Emission Depletion microscopy]] and [[RESOLFT|related microscopy methods]], he was able to show that one can substantially improve the resolving power of the fluorescence microscope, previously limited to half the wavelength of the employed light (> 200 nanometers). A microscope's [[Optical resolution|resolution]] is its most important property. Hell was the first to demonstrate, both theoretically and experimentally, how one can decouple the resolution of the fluorescence microscope from diffraction and increase it to a fraction of the wavelength of light (to the nanometer scale). Ever since the work of [[Ernst Karl Abbe]] in 1873, this feat was not thought possible. For this achievement and its significance for other fields of science, such as the life-sciences and medical research, he received the 10th German Innovation Award (Deutscher Zukunftspreis) on 23 November 2006. He received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 2014, becoming the second Nobelist born in the Banat Swabian community (after Herta Müller, the 2009 recipient of the Nobel Prize in Literature).<ref name=NP/>
