'''Nadia Murad Basee Taha''' ({{lang-ar|نادية مراد باسي طه}}; born 1993)<ref>{{cite web |author1=By Editorial Staff |title=Iraqi Yazidi human rights activist Nadia Murad gets married |url=https://ekurd.net/yazidi-nadia-murad-married-2018-08-20 |website=Kurd Net - Ekurd.net Daily News |date=20 August 2018|access-date=29 September 2020}}</ref> is an [[Iraq]]i [[Yazidis|Yazidi]]<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3ONzDQAAQBAJ&q=many+yazidis+consider+yazidis+both+an+ethnic+religious+identity&pg=PT27|title=The Last Girl: My Story of Captivity and My Fight Against the Islamic State|last1=Murad|first1=Nadia|last2=Krajeski|first2=Jenna|date=7 November 2017|publisher=Little, Brown Book Group|isbn=9780349009766|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2018/oct/05/nobel-peace-prize-2018-live-updates |title=Nobel peace prize 2018 won by Denis Mukwege and Nadia Murad – as it happened |first1=Haroon |last1=Siddique |first2=Ruth |last2=Maclean |date=5 October 2018 |journal=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1= |title=Nobel Peace Prize awarded to Denis Mukwege and Nadia Murad |url=https://www.dw.com/en/nobel-peace-prize-awarded-to-denis-mukwege-and-nadia-murad/a-45763215 |website=Deutsche Welle |date=5 October 2018|access-date=2 September 2021}}</ref>
<ref name="DW 2">{{cite web |author1= |title=Nadia Murad: One woman's fight against 'Islamic State' |url=https://www.dw.com/en/nadia-murad-one-womans-fight-against-islamic-state/a-36172104 |website=Deutsche Welle |date=10 December 2018|access-date=2 September 2021}}</ref> [[Human rights activists|human rights activist]] who lives in [[Germany]].<ref name=":0"/> In 2014, she was kidnapped from her hometown [[Kocho, Iraq|Kocho]] and held by the [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|Islamic State]] for three months.<ref name=Newsweek-Testimony-2016>{{cite news|last1=Westcott|first1=Lucy|title=ISIS sex slavery survivor on a mission to save Yazidi women and girls|url=http://www.newsweek.com/nadia-murad-isis-sex-slavery-yazidi-438421|access-date=22 September 2016|work=[[Newsweek]]|date=19 March 2016}}</ref>

Murad is the founder of [[Nadia's Initiative]], an organization dedicated to "helping women and children victimized by genocides, [[Mass Atrocity crimes|mass atrocities]], and [[human trafficking]] to heal and rebuild their lives and communities".<ref name="FM">{{cite web |title=Nadia Murad |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/nadia-murad/ |work=Forbes |access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, she and [[Denis Mukwege]] were jointly awarded the [[Nobel Peace Prize]] for "their efforts to end the [[wartime sexual violence|use of sexual violence as a weapon of war and armed conflict]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://old.nobelprize.org/pea-press.pdf?_ga=2.201315175.873660876.1538722964-1765660518.1538398809|title=Announcement|work=The [[Nobel Peace Prize]]}}</ref> She is the first Iraqi and Yazidi to be awarded a Nobel Prize.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-45759669|title=Nobel Peace Prize winner Nadia Murad|journal=BBC News|date=5 October 2018}}</ref>

Presently, she is an advocate for [[Sustainable Development Goals]] appointed by [[Secretary-General of the United Nations]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nadia-Murad-Basee-Taha|url=https://www.unsdgadvocates.org/nadia-murad-basee-tahal|access-date=19 September 2020|website=SDG Advocates|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life and capture by ISIS ==
Murad was born in the village of [[Kocho, Iraq|Kocho]] in [[Sinjar District]], [[Iraq]], populated mostly by Yazidi people.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/world/mena/who-is-the-nobel-peace-prize-2018-winner-nadia-murad-1.777517|title=Who is the Nobel Peace Prize 2018 winner Nadia Murad?|website=The National}}</ref><ref name="DW 2"/> Her family, of the [[Yazidis|Yazidi]] [[minority group|minority]], were farmers.<ref name=UNTV-Testimony-2015>{{cite news|last1=Murad Basee Taha|first1=Nadia|title=Nadia Murad Basee Taha (ISIL victim) on Trafficking of persons in situations of conflict – Security Council, 7585th meeting|url=http://webtv.un.org/meetings-events/watch/nadia-murad-basee-taha-isil-victim-on-trafficking-of-persons-in-situations-of-conflict-security-council-7585th-meeting/4665835954001|access-date=21 September 2016|work=[[United Nations Television]] (UNTV)|date=16 December 2015|format=Video}}</ref> At the age of 19, Murad was a student living in the village of [[Kocho, Iraq|Kocho]] in [[Sinjar]], northern [[Iraq]] when Islamic State fighters rounded up the Yazidi community in the village, killing 600 people – including six of Nadia's brothers and stepbrothers – and taking the younger women and girls into slavery. That year, Murad was one of more than 6,700 Yazidi women and girls taken prisoner by Islamic State in Iraq.<ref name="UNTV-GoodwillAppointment-2016">{{cite news|title=Appointment Ceremony of Ms. Nadia Murad Basee Taha As UNODC Goodwill Ambassador for the Dignity of Survivors of Human Trafficking on the Occasion of the International Day of Peace|url=http://webtv.un.org/meetings-events/watch/appointment-ceremony-of-ms.-nadia-murad-basee-taha-as-unodc-goodwill-ambassador-for-the-dignity-of-survivors-of-human-trafficking-on-the-occasion-of-the-international-day-of-peace/5128825128001#full-text|access-date=21 September 2016|work=[[United Nations Television]] (UNTV)|date=16 September 2016|format=Video}}</ref> She was captured on 15 August 2014.<ref name="time">{{cite news|last1=Alter|first1=Charlotte|title=A Yezidi Woman Who Escaped ISIS Slavery Tells Her Story|url=http://time.com/4152127/isis-yezidi-woman-slavery-united-nations/|access-date=18 December 2016|work=Time|date=20 December 2015}}</ref> She was held as a [[sexual slavery|slave]] in the city of [[Mosul]], where she was beaten, burned with cigarettes, and raped repeatedly. She successfully escaped after her captor left the house unlocked.<ref name="time" /> Murad was taken in by a neighboring family, who were able to smuggle her out of the Islamic State controlled area, allowing her to make her way to a refugee camp in [[Duhok]], [[Kurdistan Region]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Collard |first1=Rebecca |title=He Helped Iraq's Most Famous Refugee Escape ISIS. Now He's the One Who Needs Help |url=http://time.com/longform/nadia-murad-isis-refugee-omar-jabar/ |website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |access-date=11 November 2018 |language=en |date=13 July 2018}}</ref> She was out of ISIS territory in early September<ref name="lalibre">{{cite news|last1=Lamfalussy|first1=Christophe|date=22 February 2015|title=La sixième nuit j'ai été violée par tous les gardes, Salman a dit: elle est à vous maintenant|language=fr|work=[[La Libre Belgique]]|url=http://www.lalibre.be/actu/international/la-sixieme-nuit-j-ai-ete-violee-par-tous-les-gardes-salman-a-dit-elle-est-a-vous-maintenant-54e9fd2a35701001a1dfe527}}</ref> or in November 2014.<ref name="time" />

In February 2015, she gave her first testimony – under the alias of "Basima" – to reporters of the Belgian daily newspaper ''[[La Libre Belgique]]'' while she was staying in the Rwanga camp, living in a converted shipping container.<ref name="lalibre" /> In 2015, she was one of 1,000 women and children to benefit from a refugee programme of the Government of [[Baden-Württemberg]], Germany, which became her new home.<ref name=Time-Escaped-2015>{{cite news|last1=Alter|first1=Charlotte|title=Yezidi Girl Who Escaped Isis Sex Slavery: Please Help Us|url=http://time.com/4152127/isis-yezidi-woman-slavery-united-nations/|access-date=19 September 2016|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=20 December 2015}}</ref><ref name=Vice-Broadly-2016>{{cite news|last1=Whyte|first1=Lara|title='Every Part of Me Changed in Their Hands': A Former ISIS Sex Slave Speaks Out|url=https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/every-part-of-me-changed-in-their-hands-former-isis-sex-slave-nadia-murad-speaks-out|access-date=19 September 2016|work=Broadly|date=18 February 2016}}</ref>

== Career and activism ==

On 16 December 2015, Murad spoke to the [[United Nations Security Council]] about [[human trafficking]] and conflict. This was the first time the Council was ever briefed on human trafficking.<ref name=UNTV-Testimony-2015 /><ref>{{cite news|title=ظهورجريء للفتاة الازيديية نادية مراد ابكى اعضاءً في مجلس الامن وصفق لها الحاضرون|url=http://www.iraqpressagency.com/?p=175408&lang=ar|work=عراق برس|date=18 December 2015|access-date=5 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170804012507/https://www.iraqpressagency.com/?p=175408&lang=ar|archive-date=4 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> As part of her role as an ambassador, Murad will participate in global and local advocacy initiatives to bring awareness of human trafficking and refugees.<ref name=WashingtonTimes-ClooneyMuradOverview-2016 /> Murad has reached out to refugee and survivor communities, listening to testimonies of victims of trafficking and [[genocide]].<ref name=NBC-ClooneyRepresentation-2016 />

Times]]|date=9 June 2016}}</ref> to represent Murad as a client in legal action against ISIL commanders.<ref name=WashingtonTimes-ClooneyMuradOverview-2016>{{cite news|last1=Harris|first1=Elise|title=Amal Clooney, George's wife, takes on U.N. and ISIS|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2016/sep/19/amal-clooney-takes-on-isis-at-un/|access-date=22 September In September 2016, Murad announced [[Nadia's Initiative]] at an event hosted by [[Tina Brown]] in New York City. The Initiative intends to provide advocacy and assistance to victims of genocide.<ref name=UncommonUnion-NadiasInitiative>{{cite web|title=Nadia's Initiative|url=http://uncommonunion.com/nadias-initiative/|website=Uncommon Union|access-date=9 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170131081319/http://uncommonunion.com/nadias-initiative/|archive-date=31 January 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> That same month, she was named the first [[Goodwill Ambassador]] for the Dignity of Survivors of [[Human trafficking]] of the United Nations ([[UNODC]]).<ref name=UNODC-GoodwillAmbassador-PostEvent-2016>{{cite web|url=https://www.unodc.org/unodc/en/frontpage/2016/September/human-trafficking-survivor-nadia-murad-named-unodc-goodwill-ambassador.html?ref=fs1|title=Human trafficking survivor Nadia Murad named UNODC Goodwill Ambassador|last1=del Campo|first1=Carlos Gomez|website=[[United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime]] (UNODC)|access-date=17 September 2016|date=16 September 2016}}</ref>

On 3 May 2017, Murad met [[Pope Francis]] and [[Paul Gallagher (bishop)|Archbishop Gallagher]] in the [[Vatican City]]. During the meeting, she "asked for helping Yazidis who are still in ISIS captivity, acknowledged the Vatican support for minorities, discussed the scope for an autonomous region for minorities in Iraq, highlighted the current situation and challenges facing religious minorities in Iraq and Syria particularly the victims and internally displaced people as well as immigrants".<ref name="pope">{{cite web|url=http://www.nadiamurad.org/blog/2017/5/8/a-meeting-with-his-holiness-pope-francis| title=A Meeting with his Holiness Pope Francis| date=8 May 2017| website=nadiamurad.org| access-date=23 October 2017| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171028134504/http://www.nadiamurad.org/blog/2017/5/8/a-meeting-with-his-holiness-pope-francis| archive-date=28 October 2017| url-status=dead}}</ref>

Murad's memoir, ''[[The Last Girl: My Story of Captivity, and My Fight Against the Islamic State]]'', was published by [[Crown Publishing Group]] on 7 November 2017, which is an autobiographical in which she describes being captured and enslaved by the Islamic State.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nadiamurad.org/nadias-memoir/2017/8/17/the-last-girl| title=The Last Girl| date=17 August 2017| website=nadiamurad.org| access-date=23 October 2017| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171227172859/http://www.nadiamurad.org/nadias-memoir/2017/8/17/the-last-girl| archive-date=27 December 2017| url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet| user=NadiaMuradBasee| number=922532535091040256| title=Honored to announce my memoir THE LAST GIRL will be published by @CrownPublishingGroup on Nov 7th| date=23 October 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, director [[Alexandria Bombach]] produced a documentary film called ''[[On Her Shoulders (film)|On Her Shoulders]]'' that featured Murad's life story and activism.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/reviews/on-her-shoulders-review-1202679005/|title=Film Review: 'On Her Shoulders'|last1=Weissberg|first1=Jay|date=28 January 2018|magazine=Variety|language=en|access-date=19 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.indiewire.com/2018/01/on-her-shoulders-review-nadia-murad-yazidi-sundance-2018-1201917595/|title='On Her Shoulders' Review: A Documentary About Yazidi Activist Nadia Murad Becomes an Essential Portrait of the Strength Required to Speak Up — Sundance 2018|last1=Ehrlich|first1=David|date=20 January 2018|work=IndieWire|language=en|access-date=19 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/on-her-shoulders-2018|title=On Her Shoulders Movie Review (2018) {{!}} Roger Ebert|last=Minow|first=Nell|website=www.rogerebert.com|language=en|access-date=19 June 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, Murad addressed the second annual Ministerial to Advance Religious Freedom<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.state.gov/2019-ministerial-to-advance-religious-freedom/|title=2019 Ministerial to Advance Religious Freedom|publisher=US Department of State}}</ref> where she spoke about her story and the ongoing challenges faced by Yazidis nearly five years after the 3 August 2014 attacks and laid out a "five-point plan of action" to address the challenges they face in Iraq.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ewelinaochab/2019/07/24/nadia-murad-explains-the-blueprint-to-help-religious-communities-in-iraq/|title=Nadia Murad Explains The Blueprint To Help Religious Communities In Iraq|last=Ochab|first=Ewelina U.|magazine=Forbes|language=en|date=24 July 2019|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Ministerial To Advance Religious Freedom (Tuesday Part 2 of 3)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4F8ypz9D8u8|language=en|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> Murad was included among a delegation of survivors of religious persecution from around the world whose stories were highlighted at the summit.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.state.gov/biographies-of-survivors/|title=Biographies of Survivors|department=Nadia Murad|website=United States Department of State|language=en-US|date=16 July 2019|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> As part of the delegation, on 17 July 2019, Murad met with U.S. President [[Donald Trump]] in the [[Oval Office]] with whom she shared her personal story of having lost her family members, including her mother and six brothers, and pleaded with him to do something.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/07/19/politics/nobel-peace-price-yazidi-oval-office/index.html|title=Trump Oval Office exchange with Nobel Peace Prize winner highlights tension over immigration|author=Saffeya Ahmed|work=CNN|date=19 July 2019|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref>

In 2021, the [[Toronto District School Board]] cancelled Murad's scheduled visits with students<ref>https://gazette.com/news/canadian-school-cancels-event-with-isis-survivor-out-of-fear-of-offending-muslim-students/article_c70dd79e-5a02-5824-8878-0abd9f9de3b5.html</ref><ref>https://torontosun.com/opinion/columnists/fatah-the-outrageous-censorship-of-nadia-murad {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref> from 600 different schools over fears it would offend Muslims and spread [[Islamophobia]].<ref>https://www.foxnews.com/politics/canadian-school-district-cancels-speech-by-isis-rape-survivor-due-to-islamophobia-fears</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Johnson |first1=Jamie |title=School pulls event with former Islamic State sex slave over fears it would 'foster Islamophobia' |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/world-news/2021/11/24/school-pulls-event-former-islamic-state-sex-slave-fears-would/ |access-date=27 November 2021 |work=The Daily Telegraph |date=24 November 2021}}</ref> Due to public backlash over the cancellation, the school board reinstated Murad's scheduled visits for February 2022.<ref>https://nationalpost.com/opinion/rex-murphy-the-woke-rot-at-the-heart-of-canadas-largest-school-board/wcm/c574ac61-fe30-429d-80b0-522295f37bdd/amp/ {{Bare URL inline|date=December 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In August 2018, Murad became engaged to fellow Yazidi human rights activist Abid Shamdeen.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-45245173|title=Yazidi Islamic State survivor gets engaged|journal=BBC News|date=20 August 2018|access-date=29 September 2020}}</ref> They have since married.<ref>{{cite news|date=16 December 2019|first=Emily |last=Priborkin |title=ISIS Destroyed His Community—Now He Advocates for Survivors|url=https://www.american.edu/sis/news/20191216-isis-destroyed-his-community-now-he-advocates-for-survivors.cfm|access-date=29 September 2020|work=American University|language=en}}</ref>
